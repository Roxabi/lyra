## Problem

Lyra has a finalized avatar (`brand/concepts/avatar-final/006-just-solved-1024.png`), but generating
visual variations — different expressions, lighting conditions, or contexts — produces identity drift:
each generation yields a subtly different face. There is no mechanism today to lock Lyra's face
identity across runs.

Face consistency is critical for brand coherence. Without it, Lyra's visual presence fragments across
assets: a smiling Lyra looks like a different character than a neutral Lyra. The goal is to establish
a repeatable, zero-shot face-locking workflow using PuLID Flux2, with a LoRA training fallback if
zero-shot quality proves insufficient.

## Who

- **Primary:** Mickael — brand owner and AI ops, generating and curating Lyra's visual identity assets.
- **Secondary:** Any consumer of Lyra's brand assets (docs, social, UI) who depends on visual
  consistency across contexts.

## Constraints

- **Machine 2 only** — RTX 5070 Ti (16 GB VRAM). PuLID Flux2 requires ~11–12 GB; fits within budget.
- **ComfyUI-based workflow** — integration via `iFayens/ComfyUI-PuLID-Flux2` node pack.
- **Zero-shot primary path** — no fine-tuning or training required for the happy path.
- **LoRA fallback** — if zero-shot face lock quality is insufficient, ai-toolkit LoRA training (~1–2 h
  on Machine 2) is the fallback. This path is conditional, not guaranteed.
- **Reference image fixed** — `006-just-solved-1024.png` is the canonical face reference; it is not
  up for re-selection in this issue.

## Out of Scope

- Integration into Lyra's agent runtime or chat pipeline — this is offline brand asset generation.
- Automated on-demand avatar generation during Lyra conversations.
- Training a full custom diffusion model or embedding from scratch.
- Avatar generation for subjects other than Lyra.
- Selecting or replacing the avatar finalist (already decided upstream).

## Complexity

**Tier: F-full** — Multi-step workflow with unknowns, a conditional fallback path, and new tooling
being introduced to the stack.
