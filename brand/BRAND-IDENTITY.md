# Lyra — Logo & Brand Identity

## Creative Process

### 1. Starting Point: What Is Lyra?

Before touching any shapes or colours, the first question was conceptual: _what is Lyra, and how should it feel?_

Lyra is a personal AI agent engine — hub-and-spoke, asyncio, multi-channel — that runs 24/7 on your own hardware. That single sentence contains the seeds of the entire visual identity:

- **Personal AI agent** → intimate, singular, intelligent, always present
- **Hub-and-spoke** → convergence, routing, a central intelligence receiving from many sources
- **Multi-channel (Telegram, Discord)** → messages flowing in from different directions, unified by one mind
- **Always-on, local-first** → persistence, self-sovereignty, warmth (it runs on _your_ machine, in _your_ home)

The goal was to find a mark that communicated all of this without leaning on generic AI imagery — no neural nets, no sparkles, no robotic faces.

### 2. The Core Metaphor: The Lyre

The name _Lyra_ has two powerful referents:

1. **The lyre** — the ancient stringed instrument associated with Apollo, Orpheus, and the Muses. A lyre is an instrument of harmony: multiple strings, each vibrating independently, producing a unified sound. This is a perfect analogy for a hub-and-spoke agent — multiple channels, each carrying its own messages, orchestrated into coherent conversation by a single intelligence.

2. **The constellation Lyra** — one of the most recognisable patterns in the northern sky, anchored by Vega, one of the brightest stars visible from Earth. A constellation is _always there_, visible night after night — just as Lyra the agent runs 24/7. And Vega, the brightest point, maps naturally onto the hub: the central, most important node.

The logo fuses both referents. The mark is a **stylised lyre** whose structure also reads as a **hub-and-spoke network diagram**. The arms are channels. The strings are message flows. The base is the convergence point — the hub where everything resolves.

### 3. The Mark: Anatomy

#### The Hexagon Frame

A hexagon was chosen over a circle or square for several reasons:

- **Geometric precision** — hexagons are the most structurally efficient tessellating shape. They signal engineering rigour.
- **Technical connotation** — the hex badge is a familiar motif in developer tooling, package ecosystems, and hardware documentation.
- **Directionality** — a pointed-top hexagon provides a natural vertical axis that reinforces the lyre's symmetry and the downward convergence toward the hub.

The border uses a gradient that runs from teal at the top-left through amber to a faded teal at the bottom-right, giving the frame a sense of orbital energy — like a signal circulating through a network.

#### The Lyre Arms

Two curved paths rise from the base (hub) upward and outward, sweeping to the top of the mark where they terminate at the crossbar. These arms serve dual functions:

1. **Musical** — they are the frame of the lyre, the physical structure that holds the strings in tension.
2. **Architectural** — they are the spoke channels. Each arm represents a communication pathway (Telegram, Discord, future adapters) converging at the hub.

The arms taper slightly and use a teal-to-amber gradient, reinforcing the directional flow: teal at the input endpoints (channels), warming to amber as data approaches the hub.

#### The Crossbar and Yoke

A horizontal bar connects the tops of the two arms — this is the lyre's crossbar, where strings attach. Below it, a secondary yoke bar marks the midpoint of the arms.

In network terms, the crossbar represents the **adapter layer** — the point where external platforms connect to the system. The yoke is the **routing layer** where messages are normalised before being dispatched to the hub.

#### The Strings

Three vertical lines descend from the crossbar/yoke to the base. These are the lyre's strings — and simultaneously, they are **message pipelines**:

- The centre string is slightly thicker and more opaque — it represents the primary message flow.
- The flanking strings are lighter — they represent auxiliary data (context, metadata, memory lookups).

The strings use a teal-to-amber gradient, making the direction of data flow visually unambiguous: input (teal, top) flows toward resolution (amber, bottom).

#### Circuit Nodes

Small circles are placed at key structural points along the arms, the crossbar, and the yoke. These serve two purposes:

1. **Visual** — they add rhythm and prevent the curved paths from feeling like abstract swooshes.
2. **Conceptual** — each node represents a discrete processing stage in Lyra's pipeline: adapter, inbound queue, router, pool, agent.

The top nodes are teal (input state). The mid nodes shift to a transitional hue. The lower nodes warm toward amber (approaching resolution).

#### The Hub Node

The convergence point at the base of the lyre is the most important element in the mark. This is **the hub** — the central intelligence where all messages arrive, are understood, and responded to.

It is rendered as a glowing amber disc with a warm white centre, deliberately larger and brighter than any other node. It radiates:

- A soft amber glow halo, establishing it as the gravitational centre of the system
- Shockwave rings during the intro animation, signalling the moment the system comes alive

The hub is amber, not teal, because it represents the _output_ of the system — the synthesised, intelligent response. Teal is input energy; amber is resolved understanding.

### 4. Colour System

| Name        | Hex                 | Role                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Void        | `#060a14`           | Page background — deep, near-void navy                |
| Deep Space  | `#0c1525`           | Hexagon interior — slightly lifted surface             |
| Surface     | `#121f38`           | Cards, secondary backgrounds                          |
| Channel     | `#00c8e0`           | Primary accent — input, channels, activity, liveness  |
| Hub Amber   | `#f0a030`           | Secondary accent — convergence, intelligence, warmth  |
| Pipeline    | `#00c8e0 → #f0a030` | The gradient — the journey from channel to resolution |
| Star White  | `#eef4ff`           | Node centres, wordmark highlights                     |

The **teal/amber pairing** was chosen deliberately. Teal signals activity, connectivity, and digital liveness — it reads as electric and awake, appropriate for the channel endpoints that never sleep. Amber signals warmth, intelligence, and personal presence — it has an inviting, human quality that suits a _personal_ AI agent. Together they form a gradient that tells the story of transformation: raw input becoming thoughtful response.

The contrast with Valora's cyan/violet palette is intentional. While Valora's violet communicates synthesis and abstraction (fitting for a workflow orchestrator), Lyra's amber communicates warmth and personality (fitting for a personal agent that speaks _as you_).

The backgrounds are extremely dark navies rather than pure black. Pure black feels flat; dark navy retains depth and allows the glows to register as light rather than coloured shapes — essential for the constellation aesthetic.

### 5. Typography

The wordmark **LYRA** uses a geometric sans-serif (Questrial / Century Gothic family) set at light weight with generous letter-spacing (0.30em). The choices were guided by three constraints:

- **Geometric construction** mirrors the geometric mark. The circular Y, the balanced A — they sit harmoniously alongside the hexagonal frame.
- **Light weight** avoids visual competition with the mark. The lyre carries the visual weight; the wordmark provides identity.
- **Wide tracking** gives the four letters room to breathe. With only four characters, tight tracking would make the name feel abbreviated rather than complete.

The wordmark uses a multi-stop gradient running from steel blue through near-white to teal and amber, linking it to the mark's colour system.

The tagline _Personal AI Agent_ is set in a monospace face (`Courier New`) at small size with wide tracking. This references Lyra's CLI heritage — the tool is configured and managed from a terminal, and the tagline nods to that world.

### 6. Animation: Telling the Story in Sequence

The animation was designed to unfold in the same order that a message traverses Lyra's architecture — from the frame (the system boundary), through the channels, down to the hub.

**Intro sequence (~5 seconds):**

1. **Hexagon background** fades in first, establishing the system boundary — Lyra's local perimeter.
2. **Border traces itself** — the stroke animates around the perimeter using `stroke-dashoffset`. This communicates construction: the system is booting.
3. **Lyre arms reveal top to bottom** — a clip-path sweeps downward, as if data is flowing from the channel endpoints toward the hub. The direction is critical: top to bottom mirrors the message flow.
4. **Strings appear** — staggered, centre first, reinforcing the pipeline metaphor.
5. **Circuit nodes spring into place** — staggered pop-ins with spring easing (overshoot then settle), giving them physical weight.
6. **Hub shockwave** — three concentric rings burst outward from the convergence point. This is the moment of activation: Lyra is online. The first two rings are amber (resolution energy), the third is teal (the system's liveness propagating outward).
7. **Wordmark letters appear** with a subtle upward translation. The tagline fades in last, quiet and understated.

**Idle loops (continuous after intro):**

Once the system is running, it stays alive:

- **Data particles** flow continuously down the strings and arms, shifting from teal to amber as they approach the hub — visualising messages being processed in real time.
- **Hub pulse** radiates slow amber rings outward, signalling the agent's persistent readiness.
- **Channel nodes breathe** with soft opacity oscillation, indicating active listening.
- **Hex border glows** gently on a slow cycle.
- **Background particles** drift upward across the viewport — ambient data activity of a running system.

The animation never stops, because Lyra never stops.

### 7. The Lyre-as-Network: Why This Metaphor Works

The lyre is not just a pretty shape. Its structure maps precisely onto Lyra's architecture:

| Lyre Element | Lyra Architecture     | Function                                |
| ------------ | --------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Crossbar     | Adapter layer         | Where external platforms connect        |
| Arms         | Channel spokes        | Telegram, Discord, future adapters      |
| Yoke         | Routing / normaliser  | Where messages are standardised         |
| Strings      | Message pipelines     | Per-scope flows carrying conversation   |
| Base/hub     | Hub + Pool + Agent    | The convergence point, the intelligence |
| Sound        | Response              | The unified output of many inputs       |

A lyre produces harmony from many strings. Lyra produces coherent conversation from many channels. The metaphor is structural, not decorative.

### 8. Design Principles to Carry Forward

Any future extensions — light-mode variants, documentation headers, bot avatars — should honour these principles:

- **Warmth over coldness.** Lyra is personal. The amber hub is the soul of the identity. Avoid designs that feel corporate or sterile.
- **Convergence inward.** Data flows toward the centre. The hub is always the destination. Avoid layouts that scatter attention outward.
- **Dark-first.** The identity was designed for dark surfaces. Light-mode variants should use the monochrome version (dark navy on white) rather than inverting the gradient.
- **Gradients run channel-to-hub.** Teal is always the input state (channel endpoint); amber is always the resolved state (hub response). Reversing the gradient contradicts the visual narrative.
- **Monospace for technical context.** CLI commands, configuration, and terminal output should use a monospace face.
- **The lyre shape is sacrosanct.** Simplified versions may reduce node count or remove the hexagon, but the two curved arms converging at a base node must remain. That silhouette _is_ Lyra.
